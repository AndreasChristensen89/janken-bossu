How to play

Rock Paper Scissors Lizard Spock is an extended form of Rock Paper Scissors which retains the core concept but adds two new choices for players.

What beats what?

Rock

... crushes lizard

... crushes scissors

Paper

... covers Rock

... disproves Spock

Scissors

... decapitates Lizard

... cuts Paper

Lizard

... poisons Spock

... Lizard eats Paper

Spock

... smashes Scissors

... Lizard vaporizes Rock

Setting of the game

In Japan, Rock Paper Scissors, or Janken as it is known, is a very common game used almost daily to make various decisions. It is used by kids and adults alike in the kindergarten, in the school yard, and even in the work place. Japanese culture teaches people that "The nails that stick up get hammered down", which is another way of saying that people should not be too different, nor insistent on having things their own way. As a result, this can make it akward and stressful to suggest something as simple as where to eat, who picks up the check at restaurants, and who gets the last slice of cake. At a young age, Japanese kids are taught to resolve sibling quarrels with a game of Janken. The main idea is that it's a game that comes as close as possible to pure chance, and that it alleviates the pressure of decision making and leaves no guilty part to blame.

In the setting of this game the expanded Janken has become so integrated in modern Japanese life that promotions at work are settled by this very game. You start at the very bottom of a large cooperation and will have to Janken your way to the top of the hierarchy. Each opponent will get increasingly strong, meaning that a loss will result in increasingly more hit-damage. Defeat all opponents without plummeting to 0 health and you will take over as the bigg boss.
